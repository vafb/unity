The Farm Bureau Women’s Program is an integral part of the Farm Bureau organization. The Women's program embraces: The agriculture industry through education to strengthen the knowledge of agriculture in local communities, being a strong voice for Virginia farmers, setting examples of community stewardship with outreach projects and political involvement by advocating for Farm Bureau policies

Spring Conference

New for 2016 is a name change for the Women’s Conference. It will now be known as the Spring Conference: presented by the Virginia Farm Bureau Women’s Committee. The Spring Conference is open to all Farm Bureau members, female or male, who have a passion for agriculture.

2016 Spring Conference will be held March 11-13, 2016, at the Hilton - Short Pump in Richmond. Features for this conference will be updated as plans are finalized throughout the year. Please check back for updates and registration information.

Leadership Recognition & Awards

Farm Woman of the Year

The purpose of this award is to recognize a farm woman for her achievements and contributions to her family farm, community, Farm Bureau and the agriculture industry. The contestant must be nominated by an individual.

Lady Leader, 'Proudly Promoting Agriculture'

The Lady Leader program identifies women who can effectively articulate and share the values of agriculture in today’s world and provides these spokespersons with opportunities to promote farming, agribusiness and Farm Bureau.

Women’s Annual Report, Outstanding Activity and Community Outreach Form

The purpose of submitting these forms is to recognize county women’s committees for demonstrating excellence and innovation in fulfilling the Women’s Program purpose statement.

Virginia Farm Bureau Ambassador

The purpose of this contest is to recognize young adults for their interest and achievements in agriculture and provide an opportunity to serve as an ambassador for agriculture and Farm Bureau. Eligible applicants must be a member of Farm Bureau between the ages of 18 and 25.
